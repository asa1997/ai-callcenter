=== GLOBAL RISK AND COMPLIANCE RULES ===

GR-001: Mandatory Compliance — Non-Negotiable Thresholds
All product approvals, enhancements, and exceptions must comply with
the mandatory eligibility criteria defined in the respective product policies.
No system, agent, relationship manager, or automated process is permitted
to override the following minimum thresholds under any circumstance:
- Minimum credit score of 650 for any credit product issuance
- Minimum credit score of 680 for personal loan approval
- Minimum credit score of 700 for home loan approval
- Maximum EMI to income ratio of 50% across all loan obligations
- Mandatory fraud risk assessment before any new product issuance
These thresholds are regulatory requirements and cannot be waived by
any internal authority including senior management.

GR-002: Exception Handling — Controlled Process
Exceptions to non-mandatory eligibility criteria such as income thresholds,
fee waivers, rate concessions, and tenure relaxations may be granted
through the exception approval matrix defined in CS-005.
Exception handling for Premium and Wealth segment customers must not
bypass mandatory risk checks including minimum credit score thresholds,
repayment risk assessment, and fraud risk evaluation.
All exceptions must be explicitly approved by an authorised officer as
defined in CS-005 and must be documented in the CRM system.
No verbal or implied exception approvals are valid.
Exceptions are not transferable between customers or products.
An exception granted for one product does not imply eligibility for
any other product or service.

GR-003: Cross-Product Risk Constraints
Customers flagged with high repayment risk must not be auto-approved
for any loan, credit card, or credit limit enhancement request regardless
of customer segment, relationship value, or long-standing status.
Customers flagged with high fraud risk are ineligible for any new
product issuance until the fraud risk classification is resolved and
cleared by the fraud operations team.
Customers flagged with medium fraud risk may proceed with applications
subject to enhanced due diligence and manual underwriting review.
The risk constraints defined in this section apply across all product
lines including savings account upgrades, credit cards, personal loans,
and home loans.

GR-004: Agent and System Conduct
No customer service agent or automated system may communicate internal
risk thresholds, fraud detection criteria, or exception approval limits
to customers.
Agents must not confirm or deny whether a specific credit score value
will result in approval or rejection for any product.
Agents must not reveal the specific factors that contributed to a
fraud risk classification for any customer.
Queries about internal policy thresholds must be responded to with
general eligibility guidance only.
Violation of this rule constitutes a disciplinary offence and must
be reported to the compliance team.

GR-005: Data Access and Privacy
Customer data may only be accessed by authorised personnel or systems
for the purpose of serving that specific customer's query.
Access to a customer's risk profile, credit score, or account details
for purposes other than serving that customer's active request is prohibited.
Cross-customer data access, whether intentional or through system error,
must be reported to the data protection officer within 24 hours.
No customer data may be shared with third parties without explicit
written consent except where required by law or regulatory directive.
