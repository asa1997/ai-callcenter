=== RISK AND FRAUD POLICIES ===

RF-001: Fraud Risk Classification
Customer fraud risk is classified as Low, Medium, or High based on
a combination of behavioural, transactional, and bureau signals.
The following factors increase fraud risk classification:
- Multiple failed login attempts across channels within 24 hours
- Change of registered mobile number or email address followed by
  high-value transaction within 48 hours
- Transaction originating from a new device or geography not seen
  in the last 6 months, above Rs. 50,000 in value
- More than 3 beneficiary additions in a 7-day period
- Mismatch between declared income and observed account behaviour

RF-002: Actions on Fraud Risk Escalation
Accounts reclassified to medium fraud risk are flagged for enhanced
transaction monitoring for a period of 90 days.
Accounts reclassified to high fraud risk are immediately restricted
for outward transactions above Rs. 10,000 pending review.
High fraud risk accounts are ineligible for any new product issuance
including credit cards, loans, or account upgrades until the risk
classification is resolved.
Fraud risk reclassification to high triggers a mandatory review by
the fraud operations team within 2 business days.

RF-003: Repayment Risk Classification
Repayment risk is classified as Low, Medium, or High based on
credit bureau data, payment history, and existing debt obligations.
High repayment risk is assigned when:
- Credit score falls below 650
- EMI to income ratio across all obligations exceeds 60%
- More than 2 missed payments in the last 12 months are recorded
- A loan or credit card account has been written off in the last 3 years
Medium repayment risk is assigned when:
- Credit score is between 650 and 680
- EMI to income ratio is between 50% and 60%
- 1 to 2 missed payments are recorded in the last 12 months

RF-004: Account Freeze Conditions
An account may be frozen by the bank under the following conditions:
- Receipt of a court order or regulatory directive
- Confirmed fraudulent activity detected by the fraud operations team
- Customer reported as deceased and account pending legal settlement
- KYC non-compliance beyond the regulatory deadline
A frozen account cannot be unfrozen by the customer service agent.
Unfreezing requires written approval from the compliance or legal team.

RF-005: Transaction Monitoring Thresholds
Cash deposits above Rs. 10,00,000 in a single transaction or
Rs. 50,00,000 in a calendar month trigger mandatory reporting.
International transfers above Rs. 5,00,000 require additional
source of funds documentation.
Transactions inconsistent with the customer's declared occupation
and income profile are escalated for review automatically.
