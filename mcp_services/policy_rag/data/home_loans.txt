=== HOME LOAN POLICIES ===

HL-001: Home Loan Eligibility
Minimum net monthly income for salaried applicants: Rs. 50,000.
Minimum net monthly income for self-employed applicants: Rs. 75,000
based on average of last 2 years ITR.
Minimum credit score for home loan approval: 700.
Applicants with a credit score between 680 and 699 may be considered
with a higher down payment of 30% and mandatory co-applicant.
Applicants with a credit score below 680 are ineligible for home loans.
Minimum age at application: 23 years.
Maximum age at loan maturity: 65 years for salaried, 70 years for
self-employed applicants.
Applicants with high repayment risk or high fraud risk are ineligible
for home loan approval and must be declined at the application stage.

HL-002: Loan to Value Ratios
For properties valued up to Rs. 30,00,000: Maximum LTV of 90%.
For properties valued between Rs. 30,00,001 and Rs. 75,00,000: Maximum LTV of 80%.
For properties valued above Rs. 75,00,000: Maximum LTV of 75%.
LTV ratios are calculated on the lower of the market value or
the registered sale value of the property.
Premium and Wealth segment customers with a credit score above 750
and low repayment risk may be considered for LTV relaxation of up to 5%,
subject to credit committee approval and GR-002 exception handling rules.

HL-003: Co-Applicant Rules
A co-applicant is mandatory for all home loans above Rs. 75,00,000.
Co-applicants must independently satisfy minimum income and KYC requirements.
Co-applicants with a credit score below 650 will negatively impact
the composite credit assessment.
Spouse co-applicants are preferred. Non-family co-applicants require
additional documentation including a declaration of relationship.

HL-004: Home Loan Top-Up
Existing home loan customers are eligible for a top-up loan after
24 months of regular repayment with zero missed EMIs.
Maximum top-up amount: 75% of the original loan amount or Rs. 50,00,000,
whichever is lower.
Top-up loan purpose must be declared and is restricted to home improvement,
education, or medical emergencies.
Customers with a repayment risk reclassified to medium or high since
original disbursement are not eligible for home loan top-up.

HL-005: Repayment and Prepayment
Home loans on floating interest rates carry no prepayment penalty.
Home loans on fixed interest rates carry a prepayment charge of 2%
of the outstanding principal.
Part prepayment reduces the outstanding principal and may be used
to reduce either the EMI amount or the remaining tenure.
